About

Fifty years of
showing up

We started with a small team, a big map, and a conviction that the natural world was worth fighting for. That hasn't changed.

Home/About/Our History
Our Science Team Mission & Vision Partners Careers Our Accountability How We Work Our History

Verdant Horizon was founded in 1974 by a group of ecologists who were alarmed by the pace of tropical deforestation and frustrated that nobody in power seemed to be paying attention. Fifty years later, the organisation they built operates in 130 countries and is one of the most respected conservation organisations in the world.

The path here wasn't straight. There were programs that failed, funding crises, political setbacks, and more than a few moments when the scale of the problem felt overwhelming. What kept the organisation going was a stubborn refusal to give up on the natural world — and the trust of the millions of supporters who've believed in this work over the decades.

Our Story

Five decades of conservation

1974

Founded in Geneva

A group of 12 ecologists and activists establish Verdant Horizon with seed funding from three European philanthropists. The founding charter commits the organisation to science-based conservation with full financial transparency.

1981

First major field program — Amazon Basin

We launch our first large-scale field program in the Brazilian Amazon, partnering with Indigenous communities to map and monitor deforestation. The long-term monitoring network begun here still operates today.

1988

Global monitoring network established

We establish 340 long-term ecosystem monitoring sites across six continents — the foundation of our scientific reputation and the source of much of what we know about how ecosystems respond to climate change.

1997

Ocean program launched

Responding to growing evidence of coral bleaching and overfishing, we launch our marine conservation program — initially focused on the Indo-Pacific, now active in every major ocean.

2005

Food systems work begins

We expand our mandate to include sustainable food and agriculture, recognising that land-use change driven by farming is one of the greatest drivers of habitat loss globally.

2015

One million supporters

Verdant Horizon reaches one million financial supporters worldwide — a milestone that gives us the financial independence to speak truth to power without worrying about donor pressure.

2021

Climate Emergency Declaration

We formally declare a climate emergency within our own operations and commit to carbon-neutral field operations by 2025. We hit that target eight months early.

2026

Today — and the next 50 years

We operate in 130 countries with 1,200 full-time staff and 40,000 volunteers. The natural world is still under enormous pressure. We're not done.
