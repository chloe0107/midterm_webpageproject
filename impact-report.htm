2025 Global
Impact Report

What we actually achieved last year — the wins, the near-misses, and the things we're still working through. All of it, honestly told.

Home/Resources/Impact Report
BlogNewsFAQNewsletterPerspectivesReportsData & ToolsImpact ReportAnnual ReportPress & Media

2025 was a year of genuine progress and genuine setbacks. We protected more land than in any previous year. We also lost ground in two regions where political conditions deteriorated faster than we could adapt. This report covers all of it.

The headline numbers

Across our three core program areas — climate, biodiversity, and food systems — we reached or exceeded targets in 14 of 18 key indicators. The four we missed are each explained in detail in the relevant program sections below, along with what we've changed as a result.

Aerial monitoring of the Jurua Basin, Brazil — one of our primary deforestation alert zones in 2025.

Climate program outcomes

We protected 420,000 hectares of high-carbon forest — 12% above target. Our peatland restoration program in Southeast Asia rewetted 28,000 hectares, locking in an estimated 4.2 million tonnes of CO2 equivalent. Our community resilience program reached 340,000 people in climate-vulnerable areas across 12 countries.

Biodiversity program outcomes

Wildlife populations in our 47 priority landscapes showed an average increase of 18% compared to our 2015 baseline. We established or strengthened 68 new marine protected areas. We also documented the first confirmed recovery of three species from our watch list.

Food systems program outcomes

Our regenerative farming program supported 50,000 smallholder farmers in transitioning away from input-intensive practices. Average soil organic carbon in program sites increased by 14% over three years. Clean water access improved for 1.2 million people through watershed protection work.

Where we fell short

We want to be honest about two significant setbacks. Our forest protection program in the Eastern Congo Basin was suspended due to security conditions, and we were unable to complete our planned expansion in the Mekong Delta due to regulatory obstacles. In both cases we're working on alternative approaches and we'll report back next year.
