The Challenge

Land and forests are
the climate's best ally

Forests, wetlands, and soils store more carbon than all human-made infrastructure combined. When we destroy them, we don't just lose biodiversity — we release centuries of stored carbon into the atmosphere at exactly the moment we can least afford it.

Our climate program focuses on the intersection between conservation and carbon — protecting natural carbon stores, restoring degraded land, and helping communities transition to low-emissions livelihoods.

Our Programs

What we're doing about it

Forest Carbon Protection

We work with governments and Indigenous communities to put legal protections in place for high-carbon forests before they're cleared. Our programs currently protect over 2.3 million hectares of standing forest across 18 countries.

Renewable Energy Transition

We support community-led renewable energy projects in rural areas that would otherwise rely on diesel generators or coal — reducing emissions while improving energy access for people who need it most.

Peatland & Wetland Restoration

Drained peatlands are one of the largest single sources of greenhouse gas emissions from land use. We're working to rewet and restore peatland ecosystems across Southeast Asia and northern Europe.

Community Climate Resilience

We work with communities on the frontlines of climate disruption — helping them build flood defences, diversify livelihoods, and adapt agricultural practices for a hotter, less predictable climate.
