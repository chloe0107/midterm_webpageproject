What We Do

People need food
and water. So does
the planet.

The way we grow food is one of the biggest drivers of environmental destruction. But it doesn't have to be — and we're proving it every day.

Home/What We Do/Provide Food & Water
The Challenge

Agriculture drives
80% of deforestation

Food production accounts for roughly a quarter of all global greenhouse gas emissions and is the leading cause of biodiversity loss. The systems we rely on to feed ourselves are degrading the very land and water they depend on.

This isn't a reason for despair — it's a reason to act. Because if food systems are the problem, making them better is one of the most powerful things we can do for the planet.

Our Programs

Food that's good for people and planet

Regenerative Farming Transition

We support smallholder farmers transitioning to regenerative practices — cover cropping, reduced tillage, composting, and agroforestry — that rebuild soil health while maintaining or improving yields.

Clean Water Access

We work with communities in water-stressed regions to protect watershed forests, restore riparian vegetation, and develop community-managed water systems that provide reliable access to clean water year-round.

Food Sovereignty Programs

Communities that control their own food systems are better equipped to protect the land around them. We support Indigenous and rural communities in building food sovereignty — the right to grow and eat food that's culturally appropriate and ecologically sound.

Agroforestry & Land Restoration

Integrating trees into agricultural landscapes benefits farmers, wildlife, and the climate simultaneously. Our agroforestry programs have helped restore over 180,000 hectares of degraded agricultural land across Africa, Asia, and Latin America.
