Every organisation in the conservation space asks for trust. We think trust needs to be earned — through complete financial transparency, independent audits, and honest reporting on what our programs actually achieve.

Where your money goes

In 2025, 84 cents of every dollar we received went directly to conservation programs. The remaining 16 cents covered operational costs — administration, communications, and fundraising. We're proud of those numbers, but we publish them so you can judge for yourself.

Our full audited financial statements are available to download below. They're published every year within 60 days of our financial year end — no exceptions, no delays.

"Accountability isn't just about finances. It's about being honest when programs don't deliver the results we hoped for."

— Lena Hartmann, Chief Financial Officer

Independent oversight

Our finances are audited annually by an independent firm with no other business relationship with Verdant Horizon. Our Board of Trustees, which includes external members with no financial interest in the organisation, reviews all major expenditures and program decisions.

We are registered with the relevant regulatory bodies in all 47 countries where we operate, and we comply fully with local charity law, financial reporting requirements, and data protection regulations in each jurisdiction.

Measuring what matters

Financial transparency only means something if you also report honestly on outcomes. We use a set of independently-verified impact indicators for each of our major programs, and we publish those results — including the programs that underperformed and what we learned from them.

We don't cherry-pick success stories. The full picture, including where we fell short, is in our annual impact report.
