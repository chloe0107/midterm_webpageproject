About

The science behind
everything we do

Rigorous, peer-reviewed research is the foundation of every decision we make — from where we work to how we measure success.

We believe that good conservation starts with honest science. That means asking hard questions, accepting uncomfortable answers, and being willing to change course when the data tells us to.

Over the past five decades, Verdant Horizon has built one of the largest independent environmental research networks in the world. Our scientists work across every major biome — tropical forests, coral reefs, arctic tundra, freshwater systems, and dryland ecosystems — gathering data that informs not just our own programs but global conservation policy.

"We don't fund projects that make us feel good. We fund projects that the evidence says will actually work."

— Dr. Amara Osei, Chief Science Officer

How we conduct research

All of our research programs are subject to independent peer review before they go anywhere near a field team. We work with university partners on six continents, and our data sets are made publicly available wherever possible through our open-access research portal.

We also run a long-term monitoring program that has tracked ecosystem health indicators across 340 sites since 1988. This kind of longitudinal data is rare and genuinely irreplaceable — it lets us see trends that shorter studies simply can't detect.

A Verdant Horizon field researcher collecting water quality data in the Rwenzori Mountains, Uganda.

Translating science into action

Research only matters if it changes what people do. We have a dedicated team of science communicators and policy advisors whose sole job is to take our findings and turn them into briefings, reports, and recommendations that governments, companies, and communities can actually use.

In 2023 alone, our science teams contributed to 47 national conservation strategies and were cited in 12 international environmental agreements. That kind of upstream influence is how you create change at scale.

Open science & collaboration

We don't believe in hoarding data. All baseline research funded by Verdant Horizon is published open-access within 12 months of collection. We actively invite scrutiny — if our methods are wrong, we want to know sooner rather than later.

We're also founding members of the Global Biodiversity Data Consortium, a network of 80+ institutions that shares field data across organisational boundaries to build a more complete picture of planetary health.
