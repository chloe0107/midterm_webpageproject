Why we exist and
where we're headed

Our mission hasn't changed in 50 years. The scale of the problem has — and so has our ability to tackle it.

Our mission is simple, even if the work is not: to protect the natural world for the people and species that depend on it — including future generations who have no voice in what we do today.

Our mission

Verdant Horizon exists to defend and restore the natural systems that sustain all life on Earth. We do this through science, advocacy, community partnership, and direct conservation action — wherever it's needed most.

We don't pick easy fights. We go to the places with the most ecological value and the most pressure, and we stay until the job is done. That sometimes takes decades. We're okay with that.

"A living planet isn't a luxury. It's the precondition for everything else we care about."

— Verdant Horizon founding charter, 1974

Our vision

We envision a world where humans and nature thrive side by side — where forests stay standing, oceans stay healthy, rivers run clean, and wildlife populations are stable or growing.

That world is possible. It requires political will, sustained investment, and the kind of long-term thinking that most institutions struggle with. We are built for exactly that kind of thinking.

The Borneo highlands — one of the most biodiverse regions on Earth and a priority conservation site since 1991.

Our values

We are guided by honesty — about what the science says, about what our programs achieve, and about what we still don't know. We believe in the dignity and rights of the communities we work with. We believe that conservation that doesn't work for people won't work for nature either.

We also believe in urgency. The window for action is narrowing. We don't have the luxury of moving slowly.

Strategic goals for 2030

By 2030, we have committed to protecting 100 million additional acres of critical habitat, reducing carbon emissions from land-use change by 30% across our priority landscapes, and ensuring that 50 million people in vulnerable communities have access to clean water and sustainable food systems.

These are ambitious targets. We set them that way deliberately — because modest goals produce modest results, and the planet needs something better than modest right now.

🌿

Nature First

Every decision we make starts with one question: what's best for the ecosystems and species we're here to protect?

🤝

People-Centred

Conservation that ignores human needs always fails. We design programs with and for the communities who live alongside nature.

🔍

Radical Honesty

We publish our failures as readily as our successes. The field learns faster when everyone is honest about what doesn't work.

Stand with us

Our mission only moves forward with support from people who care as much as we do. Join us.
