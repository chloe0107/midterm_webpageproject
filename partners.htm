Stronger together
than apart

Conservation at the scale our planet needs can't be done by any one organisation. These are the partners who make our work possible.

We believe the most powerful conservation happens when governments, businesses, communities, and organisations like ours all pull in the same direction. Building those coalitions is one of the most important things we do.

How we choose partners

We're selective about who we work with because the integrity of our programs depends on it. Every partnership is assessed against three criteria: shared values, complementary expertise, and genuine commitment to measurable outcomes. We don't accept partnerships that would create conflicts of interest with our conservation mission.

We work with governments, UN agencies, universities, Indigenous-led organisations, responsible businesses, and other NGOs. Each type of partner brings something different — and all of them matter.

"The problems are too big and the window too narrow for any of us to work in isolation."

— James Okafor, Director of Partnerships

Institutional partners

We have active partnerships with the United Nations Environment Programme, the Intergovernmental Science-Policy Platform on Biodiversity and Ecosystem Services, the Global Environment Facility, and national environment ministries in 47 countries.

Scientific collaborators

Our research network includes formal partnerships with 112 universities and research institutions, including Oxford, MIT, the African Institute for Mathematical Sciences, the National University of Singapore, and the Universidad Nacional Autónoma de México.

Corporate partnerships

We work with a small number of companies that have made credible, verified commitments to sustainability — not greenwashing, but documented, audited change. These partnerships are fully disclosed in our annual accountability report, and any company found to be misrepresenting its environmental record will have its partnership immediately terminated.
