What We Do

35 years of field data,
open to everyone

Science moves faster when data is shared. Everything we've collected is available to researchers, journalists, and the public — free, with no barriers.

Our field monitoring network has been collecting data continuously since 1988. That's over three decades of biodiversity records, carbon measurements, water quality readings, and species population counts across 340 sites on six continents. All of it is publicly available.

What's in the data portal

The portal contains time-series data on ecosystem health indicators, species occurrence records, remote sensing imagery, carbon flux measurements, and community socioeconomic data from our program sites. Data is updated monthly and is available in standard formats compatible with R, Python, QGIS, and most other analysis tools.

"Verdant Horizon's long-term monitoring data is irreplaceable. There's no other source that covers these sites at this temporal resolution."

— Prof. David Lim, National University of Singapore

Tools we've built and shared

We've developed several open-source tools for field monitoring and analysis, including a mobile data collection app used by our volunteer network, a deforestation alert system, and a carbon estimation model for tropical forests. All of them are free to download and use.

How to cite our data

All datasets have a DOI and a standard citation format. If you use our data in published research, we ask that you cite the dataset and let us know — we track how our data is being used and it helps us make the case for continued open access funding.
