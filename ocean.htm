What We Do

The ecosystems that
hold everything else
together

Oceans absorb a third of our carbon. Forests regulate our rainfall. Rivers carry life to billions of people. We're here to make sure they keep doing those things.

The Challenge

Half of all species could
be gone by 2100

We are in the middle of the sixth mass extinction event in Earth's history — and this one is entirely caused by human activity. Habitat destruction, pollution, overfishing, and invasive species are pushing thousands of species toward the edge every year.

But extinction isn't inevitable. When we protect enough habitat and remove enough pressure, ecosystems recover — often remarkably quickly. Our job is to give them the chance.

Our Programs

Protecting what sustains us

Marine Protected Areas

We've helped establish or strengthen 680 marine protected areas worldwide, covering over 4 million square kilometres of ocean. Inside these areas, fish populations recover, coral reefs stabilise, and entire food webs rebuild themselves.

Ancient Forest Protection

Old-growth forests are irreplaceable — they harbour species found nowhere else and store carbon that took centuries to accumulate. We work to secure legal protections for the world's remaining ancient forests before they're logged.

Freshwater Conservation

Rivers and lakes cover less than 1% of Earth's surface but support more than 10% of all known species. We work to protect freshwater ecosystems from pollution, over-extraction, and damming — and to restore those that have already been damaged.

Wildlife Corridors

Isolated nature reserves aren't enough. We work to connect protected areas through wildlife corridors — strips of habitat that allow animals to move, genetic diversity to flow, and species to adapt to a changing climate by shifting their ranges.
