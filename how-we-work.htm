About

From idea to impact
— how we actually
get things done

Good intentions don't protect forests. Here's the practical model we use to turn conservation science into real, lasting change on the ground.

Home/About/How We Work

Our approach is built around a simple conviction: that conservation works best when it's rooted in local knowledge, backed by rigorous science, and designed for the long term rather than the next funding cycle.

Step 1 — Identify where we're needed

We use a combination of satellite data, biodiversity models, and on-the-ground intelligence to identify the places with the highest ecological value and the most immediate threat. We also listen to communities — local people usually know what's happening in their landscape long before our sensors do.

Step 2 — Understand before we act

Before we launch any program, we spend time understanding the social, economic, and political dynamics of a place. A conservation intervention that ignores what people need to survive will always fail eventually. We do deep community engagement, often over months or years, before any field work begins.

"We'd rather spend six months understanding a community than spend six years fixing a program that was designed without them."

— Priya Sharma, Director of Field Operations

A community mapping session in Sumatra, Indonesia — part of our six-month engagement process before any conservation work begins.

Step 3 — Design together

Our field programs are co-designed with local partners, community representatives, and in many cases Indigenous rights-holders. External expertise is useful, but local knowledge is irreplaceable. Our programs work because the people who live in these landscapes help shape them.

Step 4 — Implement and adapt

We don't just launch programs and walk away. We embed monitoring into everything from day one — tracking key ecological indicators, community wellbeing metrics, and financial sustainability — and we adapt the program when the evidence tells us something isn't working.

Step 5 — Build for permanence

The goal of every program we run is to eventually not need us. We build local capacity, support community-led governance structures, and help secure long-term legal protection for the places we work. Real conservation success means the work continues after we leave.

🗺️

Local-Led Design

Every program is shaped by the communities and rights-holders who live in the landscapes we work in.

📡

Real-Time Monitoring

We use satellite imagery, sensor networks, and community reporting to track progress continuously — not just at year-end.

🏗️

Built to Last

Every program is designed with an exit strategy — not for us to leave, but for the community to lead it independently.
